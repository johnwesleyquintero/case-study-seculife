Phase 2: Key Metric Definitions

Understanding the definitions of the key metrics is crucial for interpreting the keyword data effectively:

  • Cerebro IQ Score: A proprietary metric suggesting a keyword's overall attractiveness, balancing factors like search volume and competition. A higher score typically indicates a more desirable keyword for targeting.
  • Search Volume: Represents the estimated average monthly number of searches for a given keyword phrase. Higher volume signifies greater potential for visibility and traffic.
  • Search Volume Trend: Illustrates the recent percentage change in search volume. A positive trend indicates increasing consumer interest, while a negative trend suggests decreasing interest.
  • Competing Products: The total number of products indexed within the marketplace for a specific keyword. Lower numbers indicate reduced overall competition.
  • Title Density: The count of competing products that explicitly include the exact keyword phrase in their product title. Lower title density suggests less direct optimization competition for title placements.
