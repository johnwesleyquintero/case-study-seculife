Data Context & Methodology

This section outlines the data scope, assumptions, and pre-processing steps undertaken for this analysis, which was performed on 12 product records across 140 columns of detailed product information.

The provided dataset comprises detailed storefront metrics for 12 unique product ASINs. Our analysis was conducted under the following key assumptions:

  • Sales Rank: Lower numerical values denote superior (higher) sales performance.
  • Monetary Values: Fields prefixed with '$' (e.g., 'Buy Box 🚚: Current') represent USD currency.
  • Percentages: Columns ending with '%' (e.g., '90 days change % monthly sold', 'Referral Fee %') represent percentage values.
  • Missing Data: Empty or '-' values in numerical fields were treated as missing, handled by exclusion from calculations, or imputed as zero where logically appropriate (e.g., 0 units bought).
  • Data Freshness: The data reflects a snapshot of current and recent (90-day average) storefront performance.

Data Cleaning & Pre-processing Summary:

The dataset underwent rigorous cleaning and pre-processing to ensure data integrity and analytical readiness:

  • **Header & Row Parsing:** The first line was identified as headers, and subsequent lines as product data.
  • **Numerical Conversion:** Key performance indicators such as Sales Ranks, Bought in past month, Review Counts, and all monetary/percentage values were meticulously converted to their appropriate numerical types (integers or floats). Missing values or non-numeric entries were treated as `NaN` (Not a Number) for accurate averaging, or imputed as `0` where they logically represented an absence of quantity (e.g., no units bought).
  • **Percentage Handling:** Percentage strings (e.g., "-11 %") were cleaned by removing `%` and converted to floats (representing 0-100 range) for correct mathematical operations.
  • **Date Conversion:** `Tracking since` and `Listed since` were parsed into a consistent date format.
  • **Categorical Extraction:** From "Sales Rank: Subcategory Sales Ranks", the primary numerical rank (e.g., "# 871") and the percentage (e.g., "Top 1.93%") were extracted as separate, clean fields.
  • **Handling of "Return Rate":** This column was noted to contain both numerical-looking percentages (e.g., "73 %") and categorical values ("High", missing). For numerical analysis, only quantifiable percentage values were considered.
  • **Initial Data Set:** The processed data contained 12 product records and 140 columns, ready for analysis.

The data is now prepared and validated, ready to support robust business decisions.
